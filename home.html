Overview

The User Experience: food processors

The food processor allows its users to quickly slice, chop and grind a variety of foods. The food goes into the main compartment which must be locked on to the base. The buttons are inactive until the two upper pieces are locked on correctly. Once all pieces are locked the blade can be activated and set to spin at different speeds.

I interviewed three users on their experience using the KitchenAid food processor. The following is a summary of my observations and users' responses, and an examination of the food processor UX through personas and story-boarding.

Findings

Questions

  1. What word do you more strongly associate with cooking: chore or hobby?
  2. How would you describe your cooking habits on a scale from 1-7, 1 being never and 7 being frequently?
  3. On a scale from 1-7, 1 being unsure and 7 being completely confident, how do you feel in the kitchen?
  4. Have you used a food processor before?
  5. Choose 2-3 words that describe your emotions upon seeing the device.
  6. Choose 2-3 words that describe your emotions after using the device.
  7. Rate the interface on a scale from 1 - 7, 1 being very complicated and 7 being very intuitive.
  8. What percentage of the time using the device was spent setting it up?
  9. During the process of setting up the device, how confident were you that you were setting it up "correctly?" Rate your confidence on a scale from 1-7, 1 being very unsure and 7 being very confident.
  10. What was the most prominent thought you had while using the device?
  11. What was the most prominent thought you had after using the device?
  12. How likely are you to use this device in the future? [not likely, indifferent, likely, very likely]

Responses & Observations

User 1
Responses
  • Considered himself to be an experienced chef who enjoys cooking, had used a food processor before.
  • Found the interface to be "frustrating" and "upsetting"
  • Felt it was "unintuitive"
  • Felt that the majority of his time spent using the device was devoted to setup
  • Noted the "inefficiency" of the process
Observations
  • muttered to himself
  • laughed multiple times
  • often squinted at the device
  • "I know I locked it correctly"
User 2
Responses
  • herself to be an inexperienced cook, associates cooking with the "chore."
  • strongest feeling throughout the process was "confusion"
  • Noted she could not accurately gage the effectiveness of her actions to set up the device.
  • expressed that the interface was "complicated"
  • Noted the "inefficiency" of the process
Observations
  • "this really shouldn't be this hard"
  • stared at the device for long periods of time
  • cursed multiple times
  • repeatedly hit start button
User 3
Responses
  • considered herself to be an inexperienced chef, associated cooking with "fun"
  • Her initial reaction to seeing the device was "overwhelmed."
  • "Confusion" during the set-up process lead to "undesirable inefficiency"
  • expressed there was "really no way to know what part was incorrect."
  • Felt it was unlikely that she use a food processor in the future
Observations
  • Checked that the device was plugged in multiple times
  • "Am I doing this right?"
  • Asked for help
  • Expressed that she thought the device was broken

Personas

Amateur Amanda

Amateur Amanda is a recent college graduate who is learning how to cook. A recipe she is following calls for the use of a food processor. She finds the interface confusing and unintuitive - she spends a long time on the set-up process.

This persona was constructed from the pattern of confusion and doubt displayed by the users, and the subsequent lack of confidence during the set-up process of the device.

thinks

  • Is it supposed to be this hard?

  • Maybe I should do this by hand.

  • why can't I press the buttons?

feels

  • Completely confused

  • Fear of looking "stupid"

  • Upset at the lack of instruction

  • defeated

says

  • "Does this look right to you?"

  • "I have no idea why it won't turn on!"

  • "This shouldn't be this hard"

  • "I think this thing is broken."

does

  • Repeatedly presses the start buttons

  • Stares at the device with bewilderment

  • Reassembles the device multiple times

Multitasking Matt

Multitasking Matt is an experienced chef who likes to cook efficiently - he is often doing many things in the kitchen at once. He is frustrated by the design of the device, and particularly that he is unable to determine which pieces are misplaced when it fails to start.

This persona was constructed from the frustration the users displayed while trying to assemble the interface, and the expressed annoyance at the time wasted "trouble-shooting."

thinks

  • Why isn't this easier by now?

  • This is a waste of my time!

  • I know I locked the pieces in correctly!

feels

  • frustration and anger

  • resentment towards the device

  • increasingly overwhelmed

says

  • I have other things to do right now!

  • Someone check on my rice!

  • Are you kidding me?

does

  • Repeatedly checks the time

  • Adjusts the pieces multiple times

  • Moves the device to a different outlet

  • Shakes the device

Story

It's 7pm and multitasking Matt has just rushed home from a busy day at work. His dinner party starts in 30 minutes so he gets straight to cooking - there's no time to waste!

To save some time, Matt decides to chop his vegetables in his food processor. He runs to the counter to begin the assembly.

As soon as the base is locked in place, Matt grabs his produce and throws it in. He looks over his shoulder to check that nothing's burning on the stove.

Multitasking Matt rushes to fit the pieces together and slams the start button, but to no avail. Surprised, he presses it again -- still nothing.

He checks to make sure its plugged in (it is) and stares at the device with frustration. He finally notices that the center piece is not completely secured to the base. He fidgets with the pieces for what feels like minutes.

Finally he hears a click. Matt slams the lid shut, but before his finger reaches the start button he is assaulted by the blaring sound of his smoke detector! The oil had begun to burn!

Matt runs to turn off the heat and extinguish the fire. Defeated, he calls his friends and asks them to reschedule.
